Two‑Mass Spring Vibration System

Eigenvalues • Natural Frequencies • Physical Mode Shapes

1. Mathematical Model

The equations of motion are written in matrix form:

[M]{x¨} + [K]{x} = 0

Assuming harmonic motion {x} = {X}e^{iωt}, we obtain:

([K] − ω²[M]){X} = 0

This becomes an eigenvalue problem. Solving it gives the natural frequencies.

2. Physical Interpretation

  • Eigenvalues → ω² (square of natural frequencies)
  • Eigenvectors → Mode shapes
  • First mode: masses move together (low frequency)
  • Second mode: masses move opposite (high frequency)
